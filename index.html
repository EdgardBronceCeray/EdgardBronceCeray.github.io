About

Edgard Bronce-Ceray writes plays, novels, and translations across three languages. His work reimagines the great texts of the French literary tradition — Rostand, Hugo, Voltaire, Zévaco — for contemporary readers, bringing verse drama and swashbuckling narrative back to the English-speaking stage and page.

His most notable achievement is The Panache Trilogy, a complete theatrical cycle around Cyrano de Bergerac — the first English translation of Rostand's play in alexandrine verse, alongside an original prequel and sequel. He is also the first translator to render Michel Zévaco's monumental Les Pardaillan cycle in full — ten volumes of cape-and-sword adventure, now available in English for the first time.

Featured Work

The Panache Trilogy

Cyrano de Bergerac: The Panache Trilogy
A Prequel, the Play, and a Sequel — in English Alexandrines

A theatrical trilogy that expands Rostand's masterpiece in both directions: backward into Cyrano's youth, and forward into the silent years between Christian's death and the final act. The centrepiece is a new English verse translation that preserves Rostand's alexandrine metre — the first of its kind.
